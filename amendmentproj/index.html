THE 6TH AMENDMENT
RIGHT TO SPEEDY PUBLIC TRIAL BY JURY, WITNESSES, COUNSEL


"In all criminal prosecutions, the accused shall enjoy the right to a speedy and public trial, by an impartial jury of the State and district wherein the crime shall have been committed,which district shall have been previously ascertained by law, and to be informed of the nature and cause of the accusation; to be confronted with the witnesses against him;to have compulsory process for obtaining witnesses in his favor, and to have the Assistance of Counsel for his defence."



Translation:

In all court cases, the person accused has the right to a fast trial in a public court, by an unbiased jury from the district/State where the crime was committed, the district must have been informed by law, and be informed of the type and reason of the accusation; to be provided with witnesses against the convicted criminal; to be able to obtain witnesses in favour of the accused, and to provide the accused with a lawyer.


Summary:

When someone is convicted of a crime, they are given the right to a fast public trial, where witnesses are provided for and against the accused. They are also given the right to a lawyer. The trial is held by an unbiased jury from the district where the crime was committed.

History Of The 6th Amendment:

When the Constitution was signed on 17 September 1787, it was missing a few important sections. More specifically citizen rights. So just two years later on 25 September 1789, a group of twelve amendments were proposed that were entirely about the rights of American citizens. One of those amendments, The right to speedy public trial by an unbiased jury, have witnesses, and counsel. It is better known today as the 6th amendment. The 6th amendment was introduced to organise the judicial system in America, and make court ruling much fairer and thought through. It was to make the court system in America similar to that of the British, which had a magistrate[police] force that questioned witnesses and gathered evidence, and judges that lead investigations.

The 6th amendment took valuable key points from the 1215 English Magna Carta, specifically the right to a speedy and public trial by an impartial jury. At the time there were no professional police forces, public prosecutors or lawyers. The victim and defendant brought their own witnesses and evidence, and it was a long argument that typically ended the same day with the decision of the twelve local citizens serving on the jury. The 6th amendment introduced better police, public prosecutors that represented the victims in court, and lawyers. The amendment also resulted in more witnesses, better witnesses, and more thorough investigations, which lead to a more fair and civilised trial. Trials were publicly held by a well-informed jury from the district in which the crime was committed. The amendment was ratified along with nine others, two of the proposed changes didn’t make it, in a document commonly known as the Bill Of Rights on 15 December 1791 by ten of thirteen states.

Supreme Court Case: Gideon v. Wainwright (1963)


Clarence Earl Gideon was arrested in 1961 and charged with breaking into a pool hall/bar and stealing money from a vending machine. He was put on trial and was denied an appointed lawyer. Representing himself, he was sentenced to five years in prison. In prison, he studied law and filed a habeas corpus petition, a petition to bring a prisoner to court to determine if their imprisonment was legal, to the Florida Supreme Court. They denied his petition claiming that a government-appointed lawyer was only necessary for capital crimes, crimes worthy of the death penalty, usually murders. He appealed to the supreme court, which unanimously ruled that his imprisonment was unconstitutional and that he was to be given another trial with an appointed lawyer.


The vote was unanimous [9-0] in favour of Gideon. They concluded that it was unconstitutional because 'in all criminal prosecutions, the accused shall enjoy the right to have the Assistance of Counsel for his defence'.


I completely agree with the ruling of the jury. Gideon's imprisonment was unconstitutional, and he should have been given a lawyer. The 6th amendment says that defendants in all criminal prosecutions, not just in capital cases, have the right to the assistance of counsel.

Organisations: The Sixth Amendment Center:


The Sixth Amendment Center is an organisation that works to ensure that no person serves jail time or prison time without a trial in which they have the assistance of a lawyer and the time, and resources to provide a good defence. Most states do not have the necessary funding to provide well-trained lawyers. Some places cannot provide a defendant with a good lawyer, and sometimes they can’t even provide a lawyer at all. The Sixth Amendment Center assess the public defence systems in various regions. When they identify a lack of resources, they help the state and city courts better distribute resources to create a fairer court system.


Example:


In a case back in October 2015, the 6AC worked to combat an issue in Utah courts where many defendants, especially those facing misdemeanour charges, were never given a defence attorney due to a lack of resources. The problem is that there are not enough lawyers to help the defendants, and one lawyer may end up working many different court cases. The 6AC has been working to resolve the recurring issues in Utah’s courts.

It includes questions on topics like basic personal information, prior experience with jury service, involvement in other court cases, and military history. Examples of questions include:

  • Have you, or a member of your family or a close friend ever been a member of a law enforcement agency?

  • Are you an employee of the post office?
  • Have you served on a jury before?
  • Is there any reason why it would be impossible or a severe hardship for you to serve on this jury panel?
